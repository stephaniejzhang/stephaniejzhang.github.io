About

I am a Ruth L. Kirschstein postdoctoral research fellow in Dr. David R. Walt’s lab at Brigham and Women’s Hospital, Harvard Medical School, and the Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering, working on developing ultrasensitive multiplexed digital assays to improve disease diagnosis and detection. Additionally, I am interested in chemical and physical phenomena with translational potential in biomedical research.

I completed my Ph.D. in Chemistry with a secondary field in Computational Science and Engineering at Harvard University under Dr. Jack W. Szostak, where my work focused on synthesizing realistic models of simple artificial cells. I hold a B.S. in Chemical Biology from UC Berkeley, where I worked on the development of a hybrid inorganic-biological system under Dr. Peidong Yang.
